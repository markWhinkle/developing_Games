Friday The 13th Review

Travis, Apr 30, 2020

Friday the 13th the game is a strategic horror game where you have to use teamwork to help you escape from the wrath of Jason Vorhees. In this game, you can play as a camp counselor or you can play as Jason. Your goal as a survivor is to find parts for a car, boat, or a phone fuse to call the cops so you can escape. Your goal as Jason is to kill all the camp counselors and not let them escape. This game was very popular when it came out because who wouldn’t want to play as Jason, but another reason why it was popular was that it included a unique chat feature called chat proximity, which is you can only her people talking to you if they’re around you, so it helps build up the sense that you’re actually there. However, this games popularity didn’t last long cause the company that made the game got sued and aren’t allowed to add any more content to the game, which means that the game will never have any more new content in it. This made the game get boring after a while, but a way to have fun on this game is to play with friends on it, make a lobby with all your friends and it will be very fun. I like to come back to this game after a while and enjoy a bunch of games with friends cause they are really fun with friends. This game also has another negative, it’s very glitchy and buggy cause they can’t really update the game. Some of these bugs are game breaking bugs like if Jason throws a throwing knife at the back of the car when the car is moving, the car will fly and automatically break. I can’t tell you how many glitches i’ve seen in that game cause there are so many, but these glitches also can make the game more fun sometimes. You should try it out sometime with your friends, you might enjoy hunting down camp counselors as Jason.
